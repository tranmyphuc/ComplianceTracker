        </div>
        <div class="section-content">
            <h3 class="text-lg font-bold mb-4">1.1 Market Size & Opportunity</h3>
            <p class="mb-4">
                The European artificial intelligence market is valued at <span class="highlight">€66.4 billion in 2025</span> and is projected to grow at a CAGR of 33.2% through 2030. With the EU AI Act now in force, every organization developing or deploying AI systems within the EU must comply with its requirements, creating a substantial market for compliance solutions.
            </p>
            <p class="mb-4">
                Key market drivers include:
            </p>
            <ul class="list-disc ml-6 mb-6">
                <li class="mb-2">The EU AI Act entered into force on August 1, 2024, with full applicability by August 2, 2026</li>
                <li class="mb-2">An estimated 72% of organizations now use AI for at least one business function, all requiring compliance</li>
                <li class="mb-2">Compliance with the EU AI Act costs organizations between €29,000 and €400,000 per AI system</li>
                <li class="mb-2">Total industry compliance costs are estimated between €1.6 billion and €3.3 billion annually</li>
            </ul>

            <h3 class="text-lg font-bold mb-4">1.2 Compliance Requirements & Pain Points</h3>
            <p class="mb-4">
                The EU AI Act imposes a risk-based regulatory framework with four levels of AI systems:
            </p>
            <div class="overflow-x-auto mb-6">
                <table>
                    <thead>
                        <tr>
                            <th>Risk Level</th>
                            <th>Examples</th>
                            <th>Key Requirements</th>
                        </tr>
                    </thead>
                    <tbody>
                        <tr>
                            <td><strong>Unacceptable Risk</strong><br>(Prohibited)</td>
                            <td>Social scoring, manipulation, exploitation of vulnerabilities</td>
                            <td>Prohibited from development or deployment</td>
                        </tr>
                        <tr>
                            <td><strong>High Risk</strong></td>
                            <td>Critical infrastructure, education, employment, law enforcement</td>
                            <td>
                                Risk management, data governance, documentation, human oversight, transparency, technical robustness
                            </td>
                        </tr>
                        <tr>
                            <td><strong>Limited Risk</strong></td>
                            <td>Chatbots, emotion recognition systems, deepfakes</td>
                            <td>Transparency obligations, disclosure requirements</td>
                        </tr>
                        <tr>
                            <td><strong>Minimal Risk</strong></td>
                            <td>Simple AI video games, spam filters, inventory systems</td>
                            <td>Voluntary codes of conduct</td>
                        </tr>
                    </tbody>
                </table>
            </div>

            <h3 class="text-lg font-bold mb-4">1.3 Compliance Costs & Penalties</h3>
            <p class="mb-4">
                Organizations face significant costs and risks related to EU AI Act compliance:
            </p>
            <ul class="list-disc ml-6 mb-6">
                <li class="mb-2"><strong>Compliance Costs:</strong> €29,000 per AI model on average, up to €400,000 for high-risk systems</li>
                <li class="mb-2"><strong>Penalties for Non-Compliance:</strong>
                    <ul class="list-circle ml-6 mb-2">
                        <li>Up to <span class="highlight">€35 million or 7% of annual worldwide turnover</span> for prohibited practices</li>
                        <li>Up to €15 million or 3% of annual worldwide turnover for most violations</li>
                        <li>Up to €7.5 million or 1.5% of annual worldwide turnover for providing incorrect information</li>
                    </ul>
                </li>
                <li class="mb-2"><strong>Business Disruption:</strong> Non-compliant AI systems cannot be deployed in the EU market</li>
                <li class="mb-2"><strong>Reputational Damage:</strong> Public notification of non-compliance</li>
            </ul>
